# Lighting And Story

<!-- SPDX-License-Identifier: CC-BY-4.0 -->

Lighting supports exposure and provides a storytelling choice. Terms such as motivated and unmotivated are craft vocabulary, not technical requirements [C: StudioBinder, "Lighting 101: Motivated Lighting"].

Motivated lighting normally asks the image to suggest a visible or implied source. Project guidance: when preserving this choice in the grade, protect the relationship between the practical, the subject, and nearby shadows before applying a global color move.

Unmotivated lighting can prioritize a graphic or emotional result over a literal source explanation. Project guidance: grade such images according to the intended contrast, hue, and subject separation rather than trying to normalize them into a naturalistic baseline.

Reading shadow direction and density can help a colorist form a working hypothesis about key and fill placement [C: Filmmakers Academy, "Cinematography Lighting Ratios"]. Treat that hypothesis as a cue for discussion with the cinematographer or reference material, not as proof of the original setup.
